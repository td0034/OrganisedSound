# Response to Reviewers — OSO-2026-0019

**Manuscript:** "Evaluating Media Equality in Generative Audiovisual Composition Using Modality Constraint and Replay-Based Reveal"
**Author:** Thomas Didiot-Cook

I am grateful to the editor and both reviewers for their careful and constructive comments. The revisions have improved the manuscript's framing, accessibility, and scholarly grounding. A summary of changes is given below; specific responses follow, keyed to the editor's letter and each reviewer's comments.

## Summary of changes

- **Abstract** rewritten to communicate the study's question, method, and contribution in plain terms, and to position the work as foundational practice-based research.
- **Introduction (§1 ¶1)** strengthened with citations grounding the medium-privilege claim (Watkins 2018; Chion 1994; Cook 1998) and citations articulating the parity tension (Harris 2016; Hill 2013).
- **Related work (§2)** expanded with a new paragraph on critical frameworks for encountering and composing within electroacoustic audiovisual music: Coulter (2010), Garro (2012), Hill (2013), Harris (2016), Hyde (2012), and Watkins (2018).
- **RQ1** revised to refer to "the audiovisual experience" rather than "meaning", a term that was insufficiently grounded in the body of the paper.
- **§1.1** now attributes "intermedial interference" to Chiaramonte (2024) at point of definition.
- **§2 (Besada paragraph)** clarifies "visually salient geometric regularities" as visible patterns in the lattice geometry, with a concrete example.
- **§3.5** adds a brief disclosure that minor spelling and typing errors in participant quotations have been silently corrected.
- **§4.9** restored a missing participant quote on overload (Participant 4).
- **§5.4 and §6** strengthened to frame the work as foundational practice-based research with sample sizes that license analytic rather than statistical generalisation, and to position the method as ready for larger-scale follow-up.
- **§1, §1.1, §1.2, §5.1**: prose pass to revise machine-generated phrasing patterns.
- **Spelling errors** corrected throughout, including "audiovisuallly" in §5.1 and three errors within Participant 8's quoted text in §4.3.

## Editor's overall comments

> *The most important concern, shared by both reviewers, is the contextual and theoretical framing. The opening claim ... is presented without supporting examples or references. Engagement with critical frameworks for audiovisual composition is currently limited.*

Addressed in §1 ¶1 (privilege claim now cites Hyde 2012; Watkins 2018; Chion 1994; Cook 1998; Harris 2016; Hill 2013) and in a new §2 paragraph that engages Coulter (2010), Garro (2012), Hill (2013), Harris (2016), Hyde (2012), and Watkins (2018) as critical frameworks for the encounter and composition of electroacoustic audiovisual music. Garro's continuum of audiovisual association strategies and his contrast between Chion's cinema contract and the visual music contract are particularly relevant to the parity framing this paper takes.

> *Both reviewers also raise concerns about the abstract, which reads in places as overly technical and does not yet give the reader a clear sense of what the study seeks to explore, how it does so, and what it offers.*

The abstract has been rewritten in full. It now opens with the question driving the study, describes the method in plain terms, and closes with the contribution framed as foundational practice-based work.

> *Reviewer 2 also asks that the term "meaning" in Research Question 1 be defined more clearly.*

RQ1 has been rephrased to ask about co-equal contribution to "the audiovisual experience", which is the construct actually addressed in §4 and §5. This avoids loading "meaning" with semantic implications the study does not investigate.

> *A small number of spelling errors should also be corrected throughout, references (intermedial interference) should be checked and added, and phrasing that risks appearing machine-generated should be reviewed and revised in the abstract, introduction, and main body.*

Spelling errors have been corrected; participant quote corrections are now disclosed in §3.5. Chiaramonte (2024) is cited at the point where intermedial interference is defined (§1.1). The abstract, §1, §1.1, §1.2, and §5.1 have been edited to remove parallel-list cadences, hedge stacks, and other phrasings flagged as machine-generated.

> *I would ask the authors to reflect on how the findings are framed in relation to the study's sample size.*

§5.4 has been rewritten to acknowledge that nine participants support analytic rather than statistical generalisation, and to position the contribution as foundational practice-based research with a method ready to scale. §6 now closes with a parallel framing. The abstract closes on the same point.

## Reviewer 1

> *I would like to see a more extensive overview of perspectives on medial relationships in AV composition. The introduction begins with the statement '...models that implicitly privilege one medium...' but no relevant examples are given. Equally, the engagement with relevant critical frameworks for encountering audiovisual compositions is quite limited. Chion is, of course, useful here, but a wider net needs to be cast.*

Two changes address this. First, §1 ¶1 now grounds the medium-privilege claim with examples on both sides (Hyde 2012 and Watkins 2018 for the visual-music-as-music-made-visible position; Chion 1994 and Cook 1998 for the film-sound inheritance) and cites Harris (2016) and Hill (2013) as articulating the parity tension explicitly. Second, a new paragraph in §2 (placed immediately after the Chion paragraph) engages six critical frameworks for the encounter and composition of electroacoustic audiovisual works: Coulter's (2010) classification of media pairs, Garro's (2012) continuum of association strategies and his contrast between cinematic and visual music audiovisual contracts, Hill's (2013) interpretation framework, Harris's (2016) anti-hierarchy position, and Hyde's (2012) and Watkins's (2018) practice-grounded engagements with the visual music tradition.

## Reviewer 2

> *The Abstract reads very technically. ... Almost like it was written by a computer in places. ... I would not want to read the article because I don't know, in broad brushstrokes, what the paper seeks to explore, how it does it, and what it hopes to offer in conclusion.*

The abstract has been rewritten in full using a four-move structure: (i) the aspiration and the problem; (ii) what the study explores and why; (iii) how, in plain language; (iv) what the study offers. Parenthetical glosses ("constructive and destructive interactions between media features"; "pre-reveal and post-reveal") have been removed. The phrase "lightweight evaluation template" has been retired. Em-dashes have been replaced with commas and colons.

> *p. 2 RQ1. There is a fundamental concern regarding question 1: "To what extent do participants experience sound and light as co-equal contributors to meaning when composing with the system?" How are you defining 'meaning' here?*

RQ1 now reads: *"To what extent do participants perceive sound and light as co-equal contributors to the audiovisual experience when composing with the system?"* This avoids overloading "meaning" with semantic implications and aligns the question with what §4 and §5 actually address (the audiovisual experience as a perceived whole).

> *p.3 Penultimate paragraph: just reference Chiaramonte if that is the source of the idea (intermedial interference).*

Chiaramonte (2024) is now cited at the point of definition in §1.1: *"Intermedial interference is used here, following Chiaramonte (2024), to denote..."*. The earlier citation in §1 ¶1 and the citation in §2 are retained.

> *p.4 "visually salient geometric regularities" — does this mean striking shapes/patterns that appear regularly?*

Rephrased in §2 as: *"where visible patterns in the lattice geometry, such as the triangular groupings that form familiar chord shapes, can bias harmonic expectation"*. The Besada et al. (2024) citation is retained.

> *Watch out for spelling mistakes — there are a few.*

A full proofread has been performed. The most visible errors ("audiovisuallly" in §5.1; three errors within Participant 8's quoted text in §4.3) have been corrected. A brief disclosure has been added to §3.5 noting that minor spelling and typing errors in participant quotations are silently corrected for readability while meaning and structure are unchanged.
