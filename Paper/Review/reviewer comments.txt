Dear Mr. Didiot-Cook,

OSO-2026-0019 entitled "Evaluating Media Equality in Generative Audiovisual Composition Using Modality Constraint and Replay-Based Reveal" which you submitted to Organised Sound, has been reviewed. Both reviewers consider it a valid and interesting contribution within the scope of the issue, and I am pleased to invite a minor revision.
The most important concern, shared by both reviewers, is the contextual and theoretical framing. The opening claim — that electroacoustic audiovisual composition is often discussed using models that privilege one medium — is presented without supporting examples or references in either the introduction or the related work section. Engagement with critical frameworks for audiovisual composition is currently limited: Chion is a useful starting point, but a broader engagement with the relevant literature is needed to properly ground the study's premises and position its contribution within the existing discourse.
Both reviewers also raise concerns about the abstract, which reads in places as overly technical and does not yet give the reader a clear sense of what the study seeks to explore, how it does so, and what it offers. It should be rewritten to draw the reader in and to communicate the value and scope of the research in broader terms, without sacrificing precision.
Reviewer 2 also asks that the term "meaning" in Research Question 1 be defined more clearly, as it is central to the study yet not adequately addressed in the body of the article. A small number of spelling errors should also be corrected throughout, references (intermedial interference) should be checked and added, and phrasing that risks appearing machine-generated should be reviewed and revised in the abstract, introduction, and main body.
Finally, I would ask the authors to reflect on how the findings are framed in relation to the study's sample size. Reviewer 1 notes that the conclusions, while valid within the study's specific context, have limited wider applicability as it stands. Explicitly acknowledging this and positioning the work as valuable foundational research for future larger-scale studies would considerably strengthen the manuscript's scholarly standing.

Reviewer: 1

Comments to the Author
I would like to see a more extensive overview of perspectives on medial relationships in AV composition - the introduction begins with the statement 'Electroacoustic audiovisual composition is often discussed through models that implicitly privilege one medium: sound as structural driver with image as illustration, or image as narrative driver with sound as enhancement' but no relevant examples are given (either here or in the 'related work' section), Equally, the engagement with relevant critical frameworks for encountering audiovisual compositions is quite limited - Chion is, of course, useful here, but a wider net needs to be cast to really cement this element of the contextual discussion.
